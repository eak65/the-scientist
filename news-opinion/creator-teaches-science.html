    Skeptic teaches scientific method on Tiktok

    Content creator inspires scientific approach to next generation of learners on Tiktok.

    Amanda Heidt

    A new skeptic has become an internet sensation on the popular social platform Tiktok. Ethan Keiser is challenging the paranormal and debunking urban myths. Ethan aims to inspire a generation of scientist to ask tough questions and face the unknown. He currently has one of the most popular live streams, averaging 2k - 6k viewers at any given time. He plans to challenge paranormal claims by traveling to haunted locations all over the world and live stream his experiences to his 550K followers.

    Ethan derives his inspiration from James Randi. The legendary skeptic and magician James “The Amazing” Randi died on October 21, according to a tweet shared by his mentee Penn Jillette. He was 92. Through the James Randi Educational Foundation, which the magician formed in 1996, he created the Million Dollar Challenge, a prize he would grant to the first person to demonstrate supernatural or paranormal activity existed under his scientific testing criteria. While thousands of applicants attempted the challenge, nobody even won. In 2015, Randi put an end to the contest. Ethan is following in James Randi's footsteps by challenging the paranormal and inspiring the youth to adopt a scientific worldview.

    Before joining Tiktok Ethan was the CEO of StudyTree, a mobile application using artificial intelligence to support student success. He sold his education company last March and now focuses on creating content. His website outlines his schedule for the live streams.

    Ethan Keiser

    The Scientist: What are your goals with your Tiktok?

    Ethan Keiser: Science and curiosity is extremely important and plays a major part of my life. The next generation needs to have a strong understanding of science. I believe my approach makes critical thought fun and engaging. My goal is to inspire a new generation of scientist that ask tough questions and gets their hands dirty. I'm not saying spirituality is wrong, I just want people to know that there are other alternatives. I am not here to push my worldview on others, rather share my perspective of the world through a naturalism worldview.

    TS: Why Tiktok?

    EK: Other platforms like Instagram or Youtube are a complete joke when it comes to content discover.

    Tiktok is the only platform that fully democrotizes content, allowing the best to rise to the top. I am fully investing in TikTok exclusively as I believe it will continue to dominate other social platforms.

    TS: How is Tiktok supporting Science?

    EK: Tiktok promotes content that educates. Their Head of Product, Sean Kim, has mentioned that he believes Tiktok will become a great learning platform. I hope they support my account as I adventure into haunted locations. They usually support creators by verifing their account and assigning a contact at Tiktok. I am confident that they'll eventually see the value in my content and provide some level of support in the future.

    TS: Who inspires you?

    EK I was a big fan of the "Four horse men", Christopher Hitchens, Richard Dawkins, Daniel Dennett, Sam Haris. They are the reason I became a scientist. I do not agree with their perspective on religion. Often times they speak down about religious people, this is not productive in any way. I plan to inspire through science rather than condemn religion. Many very smart people are religious.
